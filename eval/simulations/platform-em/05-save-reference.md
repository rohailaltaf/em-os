# Day 7 — Save a reference from An Elegant Puzzle

I was reading An Elegant Puzzle by Will Larson this weekend and there's a section that really clicked for me. It's about how to approach system migrations and legacy code from a people perspective. The key insight is:

"Migrations are both a technical and an organizational challenge. The people who built the existing system often have the deepest understanding of the constraints that shaped it. If you treat a migration as replacing their work, you lose their buy-in and their knowledge. If you treat it as evolving their work — and position them as the experts guiding the evolution — you get both."

He also talks about how the best migrations are led by the people who know the old system, not by newcomers who only know the new thing.

This is EXACTLY the Marcus situation. Save this somewhere I can reference it. I want to come back to this framing when I figure out how to approach the monitoring modernization.
